# What Causes Clogs in Sinks {#concept-7482 .concept}

Informative content on the causes of clogs will be provided. This let users understand what may be creating their clogging issues in their household.

**MOST COMMON CAUSES OF SINK CLOGS**

<table><thead><tr><th>

Kitchen Sinks

</th><th>

Bathroom Sinks

</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>

-   Slow drainage and clogged sinks can slowly build up caused by fat, oils, and grease. It sticks to the pipes and catches other debris.

</td><td>

-   Hair clumps tends to get tangled and trapped in the drain which is a common issue of bathroom sink clogs. As a result, it can act as a net to catch other debris.

</td></tr><tr><td>

-   Certain food, scraps such as rice, coffee grounds, and celery, when washed down the drain can stick to the pipes, expand to block the drainage, and wrap other debris.

</td><td>

-   Soap can contribute to build-up in the pipes by combining with already present debris to create a mixture that results in a full-on blockage.

</td></tr><tr><td>

-   Foreign objects such as utensils, bottle caps, and other items that can be swept into the drain, resulting in a blocked pipe.

</td><td>

-   Residues from toothpaste and other personal care products can accumulate on the walls of the pipes, slowly creating a narrow path. This can result in slow drainage or a complete blocked pipe.

</td></tr></tbody>

First Step Before Anything

Section I

When wondering how to approach unclogging a sink, many would go straight to just pouring chemicals down the drain. But how exactly to get to that step is important to know.

Where Should We Start?

Before looking at solutions, the first step is to locate and identify the type of sink blocker you have. This will help in effectively clearing the clog and any debris stuck in the pipes as well as creating an open path to pouring the solutions.

In this section, we will be diving into 3 types of sink stoppers that can be seen in households and demonstrate how to unplug the most common type, the pop-up stopper, out of the three. Other information including more stoppers as well as links to their removal instructions will be provided.

What Type of Stoppers Are There?

Introduction to a list of common stoppers, helping users identify their drain stopper.

List of Drain Stoppers

1. Pop-Up Stopper
The first and most common sink stopper found in households are the pop ups. The easiest indicator of this type of stopper is locating whether your sink has a lift rod. If it does, then that is how you know your sink is a pop up drain.

2. Push and Pull Stopper
The second sink blocker is the push and pull stopper, which requires you to pull the stopper to drain and push it back down to plug. If you are able to do these actions, then your sink is a push and pull drain.

3. Lift and Turn Stopper
The last type of drain stopper is the lift and turn stopper, which can resemble a push and pull stopper. To test whether it is a the correct stopper, lift the stopper up and if it needs to be twisted to stay in position to drain then it is a lift and turn stopper.

How to Remove a Pop-Up Stopper?

A quick and simple tutorial on drain stopper removal.

Learn how to unplug a pop up stopper in order to start the unclogging process.

Materials Needed:

  • Wrench
  • Any item able to fit in between the sink and stopper
  1. First, locate the ball rod connected to the drain pipe under the sink. You can find a nut that is used to hold the ball rod.
  2. The nut can be loosened by hand. But if unable to do so, use a wrench to help loosen. When the nut is unscrewed, a ball called the pivot ball is revealed to be lodged inside the pipe.
  3. Before taking out the rod, make sure to wedge something in between the stopper and the sink to prevent the stopper from falling into the drain. In this example, a screwdriver was used to create an opening.
  4. After your stopper is secured, go back to the ball rod, push it down, and proceed to pull it out. The rod should come out fairly easy without having to exert much force.
  5. Take the rod out of the pipe. If any debris is stuck onto the rod, make sure to get rid of it as well as any debris that can be seen in the pipe hole.
After these 5 steps, you should be able to now fully lift the stopper out of the drain without any issue. Just make sure to insert the ball rod back into the pipe and screw the nut shut tight to prevent any leaks.

What Causes Clogs in Sinks

Informative content on the causes of clogs will be provided. This let users understand what may be creating their clogging issues in their household.

MOST COMMON CAUSES OF SINK CLOGS

Kitchen Sinks Bathroom Sinks
  • Slow drainage and clogged sinks can slowly build up caused by fat, oils, and grease. It sticks to the pipes and catches other debris.
  • Hair clumps tends to get tangled and trapped in the drain which is a common issue of bathroom sink clogs. As a result, it can act as a net to catch other debris.
  • Certain food, scraps such as rice, coffee grounds, and celery, when washed down the drain can stick to the pipes, expand to block the drainage, and wrap other debris.
  • Soap can contribute to build-up in the pipes by combining with already present debris to create a mixture that results in a full-on blockage.
  • Foreign objects such as utensils, bottle caps, and other items that can be swept into the drain, resulting in a blocked pipe.
  • Residues from toothpaste and other personal care products can accumulate on the walls of the pipes, slowly creating a narrow path. This can result in slow drainage or a complete blocked pipe.

How to Unclog a Sink Using Baking Soda & Vinegar?

Household remedy guide that requires little to no cost to use for a clogged sink.

A commonly used solution that uses the chemical reaction from baking soda and vinegar to loosen down any debris or grime in the drain. Follow the guide to learn how to use this method.

Materials Needed:

  • 1 Cup of Vinegar
  • 1 Cup of Baking Soda
  • Pot / Kettle of Hot Water
  • Measuring Cup
  1. First, make to have the drain stopper already removed from the drainage hole. Then, heat up a pot / kettle of water and when ready, pour all the water into the drain.
  2. Pour 1 cup of baking soda directly into the drain, allowing it to sit for about 5 minutes.
  3. Afterwards, pour 1 cup of vinegar directly into the drain and let it sit for about 10 minutes.
  4. While you wait, heat up another pot / kettle of water.
  5. When 10 minutes is up, pour the water down the drain to wash away any remaining solution.
Your drain should be back into working condition with no more debris and grime.

How to Unclog a Sink Using a Plunger?

No cost method that requires a common household item to fix a clogged sink.

This is a method that uses the household item, plunger, to create pressure and suction in the drain pipe to help loosen any debris stuck inside. Follow the guide to learn how to use this method.

Materials Needed:

  • Plunger
  • Pot / Kettle of Hot Water
  1. After following the guide on how to unplug the drain stopper, the first step you will have to do is to screw the pivot rod back into the drain pipe. Make sure that everything is tight.
  2. Turn on the faucet and check back down at the pipes to see if there are any leaks. If there are any, tighten the nut to prevent water spilling out.
  3. After making sure there are no leaks, fill the sink up just enough to cover the plunger cup.
  4. Take your plunger and align it where the drainage hole is. Start pressing the plunger in the sink to create some suction.
  5. Keep the motion going until you can see the water starting to drain itself.
Your sink should be able to drain normally now and is back into working condition with no more debris and grime.

How to Unclog a Sink Using Drain Cleaner?

Quick and easy method of fixing a clogged sink, but requires purchasing the solution.

As a last choice method that is more direct, using drain cleaner solution can help break down debris such as hair clumps quickly. However, this should be the last method that is used as it contains strong chemicals that may affect the pipes.

Materials Needed:

  • Drano Max Gel Clog Remover 32 oz
  • Pot / Kettle of Hot Water
  1. Open the Drano and pour about 1/5 of the solution directly into the drain.
  2. Wait for about 15 minutes. In the meantime, go heat up a pot / kettle of hot water.
  3. When 15 minutes is finished, take the hot water and pour into the drain.
Your drain should be rid of its debris now and be able to work normally.

What Other Solutions Are There?

Suggestions of alternative methods not mentioned within the guide.

LIST OF POSSIBLE STRATEGIES FOR BLOCKED DRAIN PIPE

Methods
Use boiling hot water in increments to slowly work through the clog. *DO NOT USE ON PVC PIPE*
Dissolve salt in water on the stove until it is hot. Pour the mixture down the drain and after 15 minutes, flush the drain with regular hot water.
For clogs that are not too deep, use a wire hanger and bend the tip to a 90 degree angle. Push the hook into the drain as far as possible, twist the hanger, and pull upward. Repeat this pattern until the debris is cleared away.
Another method is unscrewing the U-Pipe underneath the sink to manually remove any debris stuck inside the pipe. Make to have a tray or bucket ready below the pipe to catch any water that may still be inside. Using a scrub, clean the U-Pipe and use the wire hanger method to grab any lingering debris. Put the pipe back tightly to avoid leaks.

What Strategies Can Be Used to Prevent Clogs?

To prevent the likely chance of a clogged sink, suggested methods are provided.

HOW TO AVOID CLOGS

Strategies
Install drain strainers in kitchen and bathroom sinks to catch any food scraps and hair clumps to reduce debris that can enter the drain.
Avoid pouring anything that contains oil or grease down the drain. This can help prevent residues that will stick to the pipes.
Flush your drains regularly, about once a week, to wash any collecting debris in the pipes. This can also help in dissolving any grease or soap residues.
If using a garbage disposal, make sure to run the faucet as food scraps are being processed. This will help in flushing down the scraps.
